Mould Shop

"Qualities and skills that are important to be successful as a Mould Shop Technician are paying attention to detail and focused on work that you are doing at the time. It can be a technical job at times and also being really good at troubleshooting helps."

As a Mould Shop Technician at Hyrdo Aluminum, success is built on a foundation of technical expertise, attention to detail, and the ability to stay focused on tasks at hand. This role involves maintaining, repairing, and ensuring the quality of moulds critical to the casting process. Proficiency in time management, problem-solving, and diagnostic information gathering are essential as you navigate the technical demands of the job. Being a reliable team player who communicates effectively and works collaboratively with others contributes significantly to the efficiency and success of the mould shop operations.

Technical skills such as intermediate machine operation, preventative maintenance, and troubleshooting are at the core of this role. The ability to analyze problems, gather data, and make informed decisions is crucial for maintaining equipment performance and preventing disruptions. This job also demands a commitment to continuous improvement by identifying areas for optimization and implementing innovative solutions. Self-awareness, critical thinking, and a willingness to learn ensure that you not only meet but exceed production and quality standards, while empathy and effective communication foster teamwork and support collaboration.

Attention to detail and focus are particularly vital in ensuring mould integrity and precision, as errors can have downstream impacts on quality and efficiency. By embracing a results-oriented mindset, setting and achieving realistic goals, and adapting to changes, you will thrive in a dynamic environment. Ultimately, the Mould Shop Technician role is ideal for individuals with a strong mechanical aptitude, a problem-solving mindset, and the ability to balance technical precision with a proactive approach to continuous improvement and teamwork.

Maintenance

"A person who has strong mechanical, electrical, hydraulic, and pneumatic exposure in an industrial environment. Being familiar with how automated equipment is built and the ability to learn how the equipment is designed to operate. Be motivated to work through problem solving tasks or scenarios and ensure work is being performed safe and right the first time."

A Maintenance Technician plays a vital role in ensuring the smooth operation of Hydro Aluminum operations by performing routine maintenance, troubleshooting, and repair of industrial equipment. This role requires a combination of technical expertise, teamwork, communication, and a strong desire to learn and grow. An effective Maintenance Technician must approach challenges with a proactive mindset, a commitment to safety, and a dedication to achieving excellence.

"Two things that come to mind…first is the ability to work well with others as a team, to communicate and be willing to always help coworkers when needed. Don’t be the lone wolf. Second, have a drive to be the best technician on the team. Always wanting to learn, asking for advice or additional training as well as feedback from coworkers on how they resolved difficult issues. Desire for knowledge to achieve that goal."

The role also demands mechanical, electrical, hydraulic, and pneumatic expertise. A maintenance technician must have experience working with industrial systems and an understanding of automated equipment design and functionality. This technical foundation enables them to analyze equipment problems, understand how systems are built and operate, and make informed decisions when troubleshooting and repairing. Strong diagnostic and problem-solving skills are essential, as technicians often encounter unexpected challenges requiring innovative and precise solutions.

This role is best suited for individuals who thrive in a collaborative environment, possess a strong technical foundation, and are eager to learn and grow. Success in this role requires a combination of technical expertise, teamwork, and a commitment to excellence, ensuring the smooth and safe operation of facility operations.
