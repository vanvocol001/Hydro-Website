Saw 1

"I believe a good attribute for a saw operator would be the ability to troubleshoot problems as they occur. Another skill a good saw operator would need is the ability to work alone unsupervised."

Saw Operator 1 is responsible for operating and maintaining saw equipment to cut and process materials to precise specifications while ensuring adherence to safety, quality, and production standards. This role requires intermediate machine operation skills, basic preventative maintenance on saws and mobile equipment, and the ability to troubleshoot and solve non-routine problems effectively. The ideal candidate will demonstrate reliability, critical thinking, and strong time management skills, with the ability to work both independently and collaboratively within a team.

Key responsibilities include performing routine maintenance tasks such as lubricating moving parts, cleaning equipment, and tightening bolts, as well as conducting inspections on mobile equipment. Operators must be adept at gathering, organizing, and interpreting data to identify and address issues, contributing to continuous improvement initiatives. This role requires intermediate computer skills for navigating systems like MS Office, APICS, and SAP, as well as a solid understanding of basic math for accurate measurements, quality control calculations, and defect rate analysis.

Those in this role must possess strong communication skills, demonstrate decisiveness in making moderate decisions, and remain adaptable to changing priorities and conditions. Forklift equipment certification is required, along with experience in loading and unloading materials safely and efficiently. This position suits goal-oriented individuals who are willing to learn and grow, embrace change, and consistently meet commitments in a fast-paced industrial environment.

Saw 2

The Saw Operator II is an advanced role responsible for operating and maintaining saw equipment with increased efficiency and precision, supporting production goals while upholding safety and quality standards. Building on foundational skills, this role involves intermediate machine operation, preventative maintenance, and troubleshooting tasks, with a strong emphasis on problem-solving and critical thinking for non-routine issues. The ideal candidate will work independently and collaboratively within a team to ensure operational excellence.

This role demands effective time management, reliability, and a commitment to continuous improvement. The Saw Operator II is expected to gather and analyze data to make informed decisions and contribute to process enhancements. Key tasks include performing preventative and first-line reactive maintenance, inspecting mobile equipment, and using advanced computer systems like MS Office, APICS, and SAP for operational tracking. Forklift equipment certification and experience in safe loading/unloading practices are essential.

Proficiency in basic math is required to accurately measure dimensions, calculate percentages, and interpret data for quality control. Operators will utilize hand tools for maintenance, demonstrate self-awareness in their work, and embrace changes to improve processes and results. The Saw Operator II role is designed for individuals who are goal-oriented, adaptable, and eager to grow both technically and personally in a dynamic manufacturing environment.
