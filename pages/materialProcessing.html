Melting

"The employee needs to show a willingness to learn the areas (type of scrap to look for, different scrap profile, etc), willingness to listen"

The Material Melting Operator is responsible for safely and efficiently managing the melting process in aluminum production, ensuring adherence to quality, safety, and operational standards. This role requires a proactive approach to time management, problem-solving, and continuous improvement, with the ability to analyze data and make informed decisions in a fast-paced environment.

Key responsibilities include operating melting equipment, performing intermediate machine operations, and conducting preventative maintenance to ensure optimal performance. The Material Melting Operator must demonstrate proficiency in the use of hand tools, mobile equipment, and attachments, including payloaders and forklifts, while adhering to safety protocols and industry best practices. Accurate data collection and basic math skills are critical for monitoring melting operations, measuring materials, and maintaining quality control metrics.

Strong communication and teamwork skills are essential for collaborating with team members and supervisors, while intermediate computer skills are required for navigating systems like MS Office, APICS, and SAP. The ideal candidate is reliable, results-oriented, and open to learning, with the ability to adapt to changes and identify opportunities for process improvements.

Tipping

The Tipper Operator is responsible for safely and efficiently operating tipping equipment, including Class 7 & 8 tractors, to maneuver trailers and unload materials at designated locations within the plant. This role requires strong time management skills, reliability under pressure, and a proactive approach to continuous improvement. The Tipper Operator is expected to gather and analyze data, perform basic maintenance tasks, and contribute to the overall efficiency and safety of material handling operations.

Key responsibilities include operating mobile equipment such as forklifts, payloaders, and telescopic lifts with various attachments, performing preventative maintenance, and inspecting equipment for safety and functionality. Intermediate computer skills are required to navigate systems such as MS Office, APICS, and SAP for data tracking and reporting. Basic math skills are essential for calculating quantities, quality control metrics, and efficiency rates.

The Tipper Operator must demonstrate critical thinking, effective teamwork, and strong communication skills to collaborate with team members and supervisors. The ability to adapt to changing conditions, make moderate decisions, and identify and address basic issues is critical for success in this role.
