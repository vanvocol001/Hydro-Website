Charging Operator

"To be successful in Charging I&II, you need strong mobile equipment skills--running a payloader and a forklift. Also, you need to have strong time management skills and have a strong understanding of the process. Knowing the difference between aluminum is key in this role."

Charging 1

The Charging Operator is responsible for managing and executing the preparation and charging of raw materials into melting furnaces, ensuring efficient and high-quality aluminum production while maintaining strict safety and operational standards. This role requires a blend of technical expertise, strong time management, and critical thinking to solve non-routine problems and implement process improvements. The ideal candidate will demonstrate reliability under pressure, effective teamwork, and the ability to communicate clearly with team members and supervisors.

Key responsibilities include operating and performing basic preventative maintenance on mobile equipment such as payloaders, telescopic lifts, and forklifts, as well as conducting basic machine operation and hand tool use. The Charging Operator will gather and analyze data to monitor processes, identify issues, and propose solutions for continuous improvement. Proficiency in basic math is essential for measuring dimensions and calculating quality control metrics, defect rates, and material quantities.

The Charging Operator must have a strong commitment to safety, performing routine inspections and maintenance on mobile and stationary equipment, including lubricating parts, cleaning, and tightening components. Certifications for forklifts, telescopic lifts, and payloaders are required, alongside basic computer skills for data entry and using tools like MS Word and Excel. Self-awareness and adaptability are crucial, as the role involves coping with changes and contributing to a goal-oriented team environment.

Charging 2

The Charging Operator II is an advanced role in the aluminum production process, responsible for overseeing the preparation, charging, and monitoring of raw materials in melting furnaces while maintaining a high standard of safety, quality, and efficiency. This role builds on foundational skills and introduces greater autonomy, decision-making, and leadership responsibilities, including the ability to manage personal and team schedules effectively.

In addition to operating and maintaining mobile equipment such as payloaders, telescopic lifts, and forklifts, the Charging Operator II takes a proactive approach to problem-solving and continuous improvement, identifying and implementing basic enhancements to processes and equipment performance. This position requires intermediate technical and critical thinking skills to analyze data, solve non-routine problems, and ensure smooth operation under pressure.

Key responsibilities include performing preventative and first-line reactive maintenance on machinery, gathering and interpreting operational data, and using basic computer skills for data entry and reporting. Certifications for forklifts, telescopic lifts, and payloaders are essential, along with the ability to inspect and maintain equipment for operational readiness. Proficiency in basic math, including accurate measurements and quality control calculations, is critical for maintaining production standards.

The ideal candidate demonstrates strong communication and teamwork skills, embraces change, and exhibits self-awareness in managing interactions with others. They are results-oriented, reliable, and capable of completing intermediate tasks with a focus on meeting operational goals while fostering a culture of continuous learning and growth.
